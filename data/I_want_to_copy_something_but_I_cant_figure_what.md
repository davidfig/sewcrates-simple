<p class="normal">Many nights start like this: I sit in front of the computer and stare at a blank canvas and wonder what I'll draw. Sometimes I store an idea during the day on a scrap of paper--more regularly I have an idea but forget to write it down, which is the same as having no ideas. Most days I start drawing the Little Guy and hope for inspiration.</p><p class="normal">Once I have an idea, I either draw from memory if it's simple, or check <a href="http://sewcrates.com/index/Photos/" title="sewcrates's photo page">sewcrates's photo page</a>, or search various image searches for a model. I then start doodling away.</p><p class="normal">On the good days, I'll end up with something I'm proud of. On the normal days, I'll have something passable. And on the terrible days I'll question why I bother. As I was telling <a href="/tags/Jennifer/" title="Jennifer">Jennifer</a> yesterday, in my view, art is all about repetition until your fingers bleed in the hopes of creating something worthwhile.</p>

<p class="nospaceLeft">At the corner in the city sat a corner store,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">covered in plastic from the evening pour.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I held out the umbrella and covered my head,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">my girl pouted as her lips dripped red.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She grabbed the umbrella and pulled it near,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">I apologized and wiped her lips clear.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">A stock boy clipped tulips into a plastic pot,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">I tread past but she bent and stopped.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I reached past my coat to pat at my pocket,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">“I don’t have enough,” I yelled over the racket.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She sniffed and tweaked each petal with care,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">dismissing my call with not even a stare.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I looked through the fruits and Chinese buffet,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">And said, “bought you some the other day.”</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She lifted a bouquet from its tight quarters,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and handed to me to act as her porter.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">The thorns ripped through my leather-gloved hand,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and I bit my cheek and swallowed the strand.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She led me to the cashier out front,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">I glanced at the helplessness of her staged stunt.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">For what to do with a woman’s demands,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">but reach into my heart and fulfill her plans.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I paid for the flowers and bought her sweets,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">I learned long ago, she’s a sucker for treats.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I presented her gifts with a flourishing wave,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and she clapped and smiled at my great save.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">After many months we filled with small joys,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">sadness broke us up like two big toys.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I don’t remember what led me astray,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">But I remember her kindness of that wet day.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She taught me to woo and think of my mate,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">And never take for granted each and every date.</p><br><br>

<p class="normal">Doolies reminded me yesterday that I can post text even after my decision to horde my Horribles on the Tuesdays and Thursdays. Drawing has become rare thanks to <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Name-Wind-Kingkiller-Chronicles-Day/dp/0756405890/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1250872453&sr=8-1" title="reading">reading</a> <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Beyond-Shadows-Night-Angel-Trilogy/dp/0316033669/ref=sr_1_3?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1250872487&sr=8-3" title="too">too</a> <a href="http://www.amazon.com/John-Adams-David-McCullough/dp/141657588X/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1250872522&sr=1-1" title="many">many</a> <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Black-Powder-War-Temeraire-Book/dp/0345481305/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1250872547&sr=1-1" title="books">books</a> (that's just a sampling; I've been on a tear this summer, reading 10 books over the last couple of months).</p><p class="normal">The weekend is almost here again. This week went quicker than last week. It's cloudy today and my website says it's a measly 62 degrees. I'm happy as long as we don't break 90 again.</p><p class="normal">Last Friday I stopped drinking caffeine. I suffered last Sunday for this lapse, but the rest of the week has not been bad. I realized I was drinking coffee more out of habit than for its delicious caffeinated drug-i-ness. I'm going to focus the caffeine on my writing day (man, I wish I could add an S to that last word). I'm sipping my decaffeinated coffee right now. As I've said in the past, it doesn't taste the same: it leaves my mouth feeling a bit chalky and dry, as if it's missing a very vital ingredient.</p>

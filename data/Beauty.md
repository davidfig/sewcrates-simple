<p class="normal">-It certainly is beautiful out here.</p><p class="normal">-Yes, it is.</p><p class="normal">-You look beautiful.</p><p class="normal">-What was that?</p><p class="normal">-You heard me the first time.</p><p class="normal">-Would I be asking if I did?</p><p class="normal">-Uh huh. I know you heard me the first time.</p><p class="normal">-What did you say?</p><p class="normal">-I said you look beautiful out here.</p><p class="normal">-Oh, only out here?</p><p class="normal">-You know what I meant.</p><p class="normal">-No, not really. But I do feel beautiful out here. The moon reflecting off the water, it draws your eye. It makes me feel pretty, you know. It’s nice to feel pretty once in a while.</p><p class="normal">-But you’re beautiful all the time. Why would you feel pretty only now in the moonlight?</p><p class="normal">-It’s nice of you to say.</p><p class="normal">-I wouldn’t make that up.</p><p class="normal">-As I said, it’s nice of you to say. It’s something you don’t say enough, and it’s good to hear you practicing. You should try it more. It makes a girl feel all warmed inside.</p><p class="normal">-I’m not practicing—it’s what I think of you. Look, you are every bit as beautiful as the moon and water and the moon reflecting on the water. It’s crazy that you don’t see it.</p><p class="normal">- I appreciate your feeble attempts at poetry. And, for the record, I never said that I didn’t see it.</p><p class="normal">-So then you know you’re beautiful? Why did you put me through all of that? What were you trying to prove?</p><p class="normal">-It doesn’t work that way. Why do you always think I’m putting you through something? Or that everything I say is about you? You should know better than that. How I see my beauty is very complicated. And it’s not something that I can prove to you.</p><p class="normal">-I only talk empirically. You are without doubt empirically beautiful. If you like I’ll set up some experiments to determine the percentages. That way you won’t have any doubts.</p><p class="normal">-I don’t know why you think my doubts are empirical. You always think and talk empirically. I don’t feel or see based on your studies or averages. I only speak about how it really feels in my own head and through my own eyes. Not everything is external and measurable. Some things are internal.</p><p class="normal">-Well I can’t speak to your insides, but your eyes are beautiful, and through them you must see how pretty you are. Not all the moon rises in the world would compare.</p><p class="normal">-See? Now you’re just setting me up to take advantage of.</p><p class="normal">-Do you say that based on empirical evidence?</p><p class="normal">-I don’t need empirical anything to know where you’re heading with this.</p><p class="normal">-Then you wouldn’t mind helping me with my next move then.</p><p class="normal">-You’re incorrigible.</p><p class="normal">-Smart and beautiful. How did I ever get so lucky?</p>

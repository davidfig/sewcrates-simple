<p class="normal">Here is the list of words that are used for the greetings before and after Naginata practice.</p><p class="normal"><span class="underline">Before practice</span>:</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Chakuza (kneel down)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Mokusou (meditate)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Yame (end meditation)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Shomen-ni,  Rei (face the Shomen, bow)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Sensei/Senseigata*-ni,  Rei (face the Sensei, bow)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Otagai-ni,  Rei (final bow)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">(Onegai shimasu) (response)</p><p class="normal"><span class="underline">After practice</span>:</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Chakuza (kneel down)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Mokusou (meditate)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Yame (end meditation)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Sensei/Senseigata*-ni,  Rei (face the Sensei, bow)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Shomen-ni,  Rei (face the Shomen, bow)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Otagai-ni,  Rei (final bow)</p><p class="nospaceLeft">(Arigatou gozaimashita) (response)</p><p class="normal">Senseigata is a polite plural form of Sensei.</p>

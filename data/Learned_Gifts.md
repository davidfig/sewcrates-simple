<p class="nospaceLeft"> At the corner in the city sat a corner store,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> covered in plastic to keep out the evening pour.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"> I held the umbrella to cover my head,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> She pouted to indicate her lips dripping red.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"> A man clipped tulips into a plastic pot,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> I tread past but she bent and stopped.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"> I reached past my coat and patted at my pocket,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> “I don’t have enough,” I yelled over the racket.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"> She sniffed and tweaked each petal with care,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> dismissing my call with an uneven stare.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"> I looked through the fruits and Chinese buffet,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> “I bought you flowers just the other day.”</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"> “You didn’t,” she said through the rain-soaked plastic,</p><p class="nospaceLeft"> “Oh, that’s right,” I said, “I must be spastic.”</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She lifted a bouquet from its tight quarters,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and handed them to me to act as her porter.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">The thorns ripped through my leather-gloved hand,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and I bit my cheek and swallowed skin’s strand.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">She led me to the cashier waiting out front,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">I glanced at the helplessness of her staged stunt.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">For what to do with a woman’s demands,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">but reach into my heart and fulfill her plans.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I paid for the flowers and bought her sweets,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">I learned long ago that she’s a sucker for treats.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I presented her gifts with a flourishing wave,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and she clapped and smiled at my great save.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">We met in passing just the other day,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and we chatted it up and I made her stay.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">We harked back to our moments of joy,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">before sadness broke us like two big toys.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">“The gifts I remember you taught me to buy,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">without which no girl would have grown nigh.”</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">We laughed remembering what led me astray,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and remembered the kindness of that wet day.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I told her how happy I am with my girl,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">she regretted that we didn’t give it a whirl.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I smiled and said, “Think of that night.”</p><p class="nospaceLeft">“It would be years before I got over that sight.”</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I waved goodbye to return to my girl Sue,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">“It was because of you I finally learned to woo.”</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">I think back toward the lessons she taught</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and finally realize that nothing was for naught.</p><br><br><p class="center">***</p><p class="normal"><span class="italics">Yeah, tiredness found me. I might rework it when I find a chance. It's not up to my usual standards of bad poems.</span></p>

<p class="normal">Depression stalks me. I’m easy prey. After a lackluster weekend, I find myself with bad brain chemistry. I’m lethargic. I type this with my head leaning to the left as if I’m pretending to listen to some distant sound. I have a cup of caffeine next to me that I have not bothered to drink. I’m not even sure I want any liquid gold. Yes, it’s that bad, and not just bad but inexplicably bad.</p><p class="normal">I missed my van this morning. It wasn’t because I forgot to set the clocks forward. Instead, I worried so much about setting my clocks forward that I forgot to check if I had set my alarm (I hadn’t—it was still set for my van-free Friday commute).</p><p class="normal">Enough of the complaining. I sip the black goodness mixed with rainwater and feel its spell.</p><p class="normal">Starting from a blank page. Opening up and seeing where this takes me. I have nowhere to be and anywhere to go. For now, I’ll just tap away and watch.</p><p class="normal">The caffeine slowly works through my stomach and my blood absorbs it, and sends it to my brain. My focus increases and I wait for wisdom or at least some semblance of it to pass the time.</p><p class="normal">Over the last few days, I’ve been working on another story. Nothing to post today, though. I’m hoping to finish it up later in the week. And, yes, I am using this as a break between my last bad doodle and today’s depressed doodle. Try not to blind yourself. Enjoy.</p>

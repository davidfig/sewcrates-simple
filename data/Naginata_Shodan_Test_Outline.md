Shiai competition</p><ul><li> bogu is worn<li> try to hit your opponent on a valid datosu-bui<li> while avoiding being hit<li> competition continues until someone meets the victory conditions (e.g., two valid strikes) or time runs out</ul><br><p class="nospaceLeft">2. Engi competition</p><ul><li> Movements are pre-arranged<li> Either shikaki-oji competition or Zen Nihon kata competition<li> Winner is pair that is judged to have the best skill</ul><br><p class="nospaceLeft"><span class="underline">Names of the footwork</span></p><p class="nospaceLeft">Okuri-ashi</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Ayumi-ashi</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Hiraki-ashi</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Famikae-ashi</p><p class="nospaceLeft">Tsugi-ashi</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft"><span class="underline">Ma-ai ga Chigai</span> (close distance)</p><ul><li> Mai-ai is the distance betweem you and your opponent<li> correct distance = 1 step forward to hit your opponent & 1 step backward to avoid being hit<li> ma-ai ga chigai is a mai-ai that is closer than the correct ma-ai<li> if you can't keep ma-ai and you get too close, you are easier to hit</ul><br><p class="nospaceLeft"><span class="underline">Distance between starting lines in Engi and Shiai</span></p><p class="nospaceLeft"> 4m in both Engi and Shiai</p>

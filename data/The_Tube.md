<p class="nospaceLeft">Rats, roaches and sardines all squeezed in tight,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">move to the right or they will push and fight.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Double circle of red slashed in the middle;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">to travel underground you space out little.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Tourists all stand in a crowd, gaze and gasp,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">watching as locals an chaps whisk on past.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">The tracks begin to rattle and light is seen,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">off in the distance is the train’s shrill keen.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Doors fly open, we push and run inside,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">lunge for a seat before a change in tide.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">The train begins to lurch, the scenery pass,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">one stop then another as crowds start to mass;</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">The heat and your sweat slowly down it pours,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">with a lovely odor wafting next door.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Transfer to green, red, purple, and to blue,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">arrive at your station and get your due.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Ticket in machine, and then out it pops,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">eyes on the wall or you’ll miss your stop.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Your trip is all done and your pounds are paid,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">break for the sunlight and watch the parade.</p><br>

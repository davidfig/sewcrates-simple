<p class="nospaceLeft">electrons zap greetings, exchanges;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">new zaps follow silence, until:</p><p class="nospaceLeft">meet in steak’s house, jack-o’-lanterns near.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">opera’s next, but i know it can’t last;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">not a relationship man;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">never been one—didn’t know how.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">‘moving away,’ they tell me, ‘far away’;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">‘where am i going to poke,’ she asks;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">‘somewhere beautiful, sunny and sandy.’</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">she goes, leaving me humid and dreadful;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">no go for me, thunder rolls;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">classes on ardor, i learn slowly.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">year plus-plus passes, ears pop often;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">eating headsets like candy;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">soreness of distance and pretzel rest.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">heart quickens when i say ‘wah i nee’ ;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">‘wah she-ong nee’ we cry, ‘wah she-ong nee’ ;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">story’s unfinished, beginnings await.</p><br>

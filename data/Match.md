<p class="normal">In the early breeze of autumn, when moisture sculpts the air, a tree lists, dipping a naked root in a pregnant river.</p><p class="normal">The root clutches warily a bobbing spoil, its wooden fingers clinging against the river's strain.</p><p class="normal">Instance: the prize fancies the grasp, speculates, and discovers the tree leaning forth.</p><p class="normal">The two share harbor from the river, entangle, feed, and grow together.</p><p class="normal">But as rivers tend to flow, and resolves weaken, root, whipped by the violent water, may snap, freeing the prize.</p><p class="normal">As the tree grows—and craves—there follows, often belatedly, a fresh root willing to test the waters of fate.</p>

<p class="normal">—Serge, the sky is green today.</p><p class="normal">—That is unusual. I’ve seen it pink and maybe orange or red, but mostly blue and at night, black, but I worry to call it green.</p><p class="normal">—That big space over there, <span class="italics">we</span> call it sky, look to it, not to me, that covering, that film, that sparkled gradient, that is green.</p><p class="normal">—I’m sorry sir, but isn’t that blue? Not to be difficult, I mean to give no offense, but it’s cloudless and clear to forever.</p><p class="normal">—Nevertheless green as meadows, as scum-covered ponds, as camouflaged trucks, as forest canopies, as the middle name of Roy Biv.</p><p class="normal">—I think I see it now, yes, definitely green. My denseness is at times a crutch, sir.</p><p class="normal">—Spread the truth of this matter.</p><p class="normal">—As you say.</p>

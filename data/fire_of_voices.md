<p class="nospaceLeft">It was better when the fire of voices were suppressed;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">when moments affected me with cold calculation.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">When anger, hatred, frustration, sadness could be held at bay,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">at little more than the cost of happiness and love.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">When only tidal forces could breach my fortress,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and, once breached, would wash away the receding water.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">Rational thoughts were clouded less, and rages were few,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">except with blood—when built up gases would explode forth on those most familiar and least deserving.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">It was better when emotions were suppressed because of memory of pains;</p><p class="nospaceLeft">when personalities could be pulled over my face like a poorly fit mask.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">When lies and truth mattered little to me,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and each was exchanged for little more than a passing fancy.</p><br><p class="nospaceLeft">But with worse comes happiness and love,</p><p class="nospaceLeft">and isn’t the fire of voices but a small price to pay for truly living?</p><br>
